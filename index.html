Enabling children to build AI programs

The eCraft2Learn project is developing a set of extensions to the Snap! programming language to enable children (and non-expert programmers) to build AI programs.

A teacherâ€™s guide that describes the extensions, projects, and the larger context currently consists of four chapters:

  1. Speech Output
  2. Speech Recognition
  3. Image Recognition
  4. Machine Learning
An early version of the extension is described in the paper Child-friendly Programming Interfaces to AI Cloud Services by Ken Kahn and Niall Winters.

The following demonstration programs use the AI Snap! blocks. They run best in the Chrome browser. Examples 1 to 7 require a speaker or headphones. Examples 1 through 5 also require a microphone. Examples 6 and 7 also require a camera and API keys. Example 8 and 9 require only a camera.

  1. Speak with random pitch, rate, voice, and language
  2. Speak commands to a sprite
  3. Funny templated sentences
  4. Story generator (new)
  5. Talk to Wikipedia (new)
  6. Says what it sees
  7. Says what it sees when asked to
  8. After training the turtle moves depending on which way your finger is pointed (new)
  9. After training the turtle moves left or right depending on which way you lean (new)
