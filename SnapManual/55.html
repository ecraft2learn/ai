55 F. Hyperblocks A scalar is anything other than a list. The name comes from mathematics, where it means a magnitude without direction, as opposed to a vector, which points toward somewhere. A scalar function is one whose domain and range are scalars, so all the arithmetic operations are scalar functions, but so are the text ones such as leer and the Boolean ones such as not.
