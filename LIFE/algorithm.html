Pneumonia_COVID-19 Clinical Decision Algorithm for children aged 1 month to 5 years

History of Fever and either of Cough or Difficulty in Breathing PLUS either a positive history of contact for COVID-19 in the last 14 days prior to illness or from a location with confirmed cases due to community transmission.

IF NO, send to Main (Paediatric) OPD
IF YES
↓

Activate COVID-19 Emergency Operations Plan by:

✓ Alert and send child to designated Acute Respiratory Illness (ARI) with suspected COVID-19 OPD Isolation area (Red and Orange zone)
✓ All patients must maintain ≥1meter distance from each other.
✓ Staff to adhere to mandatory hand hygiene and appropriate PPE (gown, eye goggles, face mask and gloves)
✓ All children (if can tolerate) and their caregivers to be given a surgical mask to wear immediately before triage.
✓ Triage each patient at the designated isolation area (Suspected COVID-19) of the waiting bay for signs of severe illness (emergency signs)
✓Move emergency and priority cases to the examination room to perform ABCDE assessment. Examination bed/couch to be fumigated in between patients.
✓Keep limited duration in the examination room.
✓Surgical mask or N95 mask (where possible, full PPE) should be worn during ABCDE assessment.

Severe pneumonia if any of

1) Grunting (+/- Very Severe chest wall in-drawing)
2) Oxygen saturation less than 90% (or central cyanosis)
3) Inability or refusal to feed
4) AVPU <A, Lethargy

✓ Classify as unwell (high risk) AND immediately,
✓ Alert and send the child to designated COVID-19 isolation in-patient unit for admission.
✓ If the cubicle/unit holds ≥1 patient, adhere to >1meter distance from each bed.
✓ Admit in the isolation cubicle for:
1) Low Flow Oxygen therapy at 0.5-2L/min via nasal prongs or cannula. Use Non-rebreather mask if infant tolerates or for older children.
2) Do not exceed 2L/min for nasal prongs/cannula. Do not use nasopharyngeal airway (high risk of aerosal spread)
3) Target >90-94%.
4) If has a wheeze, add salbutamol inhaler via MDI/spacer (do not nebulise). Do not give systemic steroids unless if known or suspected asthmatic attack.
5) Give antipyretics (paracetamol)
6) Start on broad-spectrum antibiotics (Xpen/gentamycin)

1) Reassure families and educate on infection prevention measures while handling the child
2) Only one carer is allowed per child. Visitors should not be allowed.
3) Promote breastfeeding and nutrition
4) Use N95 masks before high-risk procedures (suctioning, bagging, naso-pharyngeal swabbing)
5) Collect sample and immediately send for SARS-CoV-2 PCR processing
6) Maintenance fluids are allowed. Do not routinely give fluid boluses unless indicated.
7) Consider HDU/ICU care for CPAP or ventilator support if still requires oxygen by day 3 or has respiratory failure
8) Monitor signs of organ failure and consult senior review urgently
9) Routine steroids, anti-viral agents and chest X-ray is not recommended

Ref: WHO, (March 13th 2020) Clinical Management of Severe Acute Respiratory Infection (SARI) When COVID-19 Disease is suspected. Available at https://www.who.int/publications-detail/clinical-management-of-severe-acute-respiratory-infection-when-novel-coronavirus-(ncov)-infection-is-suspected
